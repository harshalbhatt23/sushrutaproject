                        aṣṭarūpam akhilasyāsya jagataḥ saṃbhavahetur avyaktan nāma | tad
                        ekam anekeṣāṃ kṣetrajñānām adhiṣṭhānaṃ samudra ivodakaujasāṃ

                    <ab corresp="#SS.3.1.3" type="apparatus">
                        <list type="Notes">
                            <item corresp="#SS.3.1.3-n1">Witnesses N and H read <foreign
                                    xml:lang="sa">samudra ivodakaujasāṃ
                                bhāvānām</foreign>, which is an unusual expression. It
                                might mean "like the ocean is a support for creatures that
                                take their strength (<foreign xml:lang="sa"
                                >ojas</foreign>) from water." The simile is that The One
                                Unmanifest is the foundation of all conscious beings just
                                like the ocean is for creatures that live in the sea.
                                Ḍalhaṇa remarked that there were different interpretations
                                of this simile in the vulgate version, <foreign
                                    xml:lang="sa">ivaudakānām</foreign>. Some thought it
                                meant ``like rivers, lakes and other forms of water are
                                supported by the ocean", while other thought it referred
                                to living beings like fish and plants that are supported
                                by the ocean." It is tempting to consider an emendation,
                                against all the Nepalese witnesses, to <foreign
                                    xml:lang="sa">samudra ivodakajānāṃ</foreign> ``like
                                the ocean for beings that are born in the waters,'' which
                                means the same as the vulgate reading.</item>
                        </list>
                    </ab>
